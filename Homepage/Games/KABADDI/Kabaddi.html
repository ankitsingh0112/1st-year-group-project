~About

  • Kabaddi is a contact game team sport. Played between two teams of seven players, the objective of the game is for a single player on offence, referred to as a "raider", to run into the opposing team's half of a court, touch out as many of their defenders as possible, and return to their own half of the court, all without being tackled by the defenders, and in a single breath.

    • ~Rules

  • Kabaddi mat dimensions may vary according to the tournaments and age groups, but it is mostly measures 13m *10m for senior men's professional kabaddi events. The mat is slightly smaller, 12m*8m, for women.
  • In each half, there are two more lines drawn parallel to the mid line. The baulk line is at a distance of 3.75m from the mid line while the bonus line is drawn 1m further back from the baulk line.
  • A kabaddi match typically runs for 40 minutes (two halvesof 20 minutes each).
  • Each team in a kabaddi match has seven players. The team can also have three to five substitute players on the bench.
  • Raider can either go for touch points,which means he tags one or more of the opposition players and succesfully escapes to his own half without breaking the cant. If touch points are scored, the antis or defenders who had been tagged during the raid have to exit the mat. Raiders earn as many touch points as the number of the players they eliminate during a raid.
  • Similarly, a raider is also out of the game if they are tackled by the opposition defenders during a raid. A successful tackle earns the defending team one point.
  • The other way for raiders to score is through bonus points.To score a bonus point, a raider needs to plant one foot across the bonus line while having the trailing foot on air. Bonus points, however, are only active when the defending team has six or more players on the mat.
  • The eliminated players, however can be revived and brought back into the game if a raider from their team scores touch points on the opposition team during a subsequent raid or their team can pull off a succesful raid on the opposition raider as the match progress.
  • The players shall be declared out if any of his body part touches outside the court or if any player goes ourt of the end lines during the course of play. If one or more defenders who have gone out of the boundary holding the raiders shall be declared out but the raider will not be out.

    ~Fouls

  • Stifle the raider by shutting his mouth in any way.
  • Violent tackling which leads to injury.
  • Hold raider with the help of scissors made by legs
  • Holding the jersy of raider while his run to his half.
  • Taking more than 5 seconds to start the raid.
  • Preventing the raider takes his turn of raid.
  • Coaching from outside the palaying area.
